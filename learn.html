Fitness Terminology

Body Mass Index (BMI)

BMI is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It's calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in meters.

BMI Categories:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5-24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25-29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or higher

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

TDEE is the total number of calories your body burns in a day, including all activities. It's calculated using your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and activity level.

Activity Level Multipliers:

  • Sedentary: 1.2 (little or no exercise)
  • Lightly Active: 1.375 (light exercise 1-3 days/week)
  • Moderately Active: 1.55 (moderate exercise 3-5 days/week)
  • Very Active: 1.725 (hard exercise 6-7 days/week)
  • Extra Active: 1.9 (very hard exercise & physical job)

Macronutrients

Macronutrients are the nutrients your body needs in large amounts: proteins, carbohydrates, and fats.

Protein

Essential for muscle building and repair. Recommended: 1.6-2.2g per kg of body weight.

Carbohydrates

Main energy source. Recommended: 45-65% of total daily calories.

Fats

Essential for hormone production and nutrient absorption. Recommended: 20-35% of total daily calories.

Training Terms

Reps & Sets

Reps (repetitions) are the number of times you perform an exercise. Sets are groups of reps with rest periods in between.

Progressive Overload

Gradually increasing the weight, frequency, or number of repetitions in your training routine.

Rest Periods

The time taken between sets to allow muscles to recover. Typically 30-90 seconds for hypertrophy, 2-5 minutes for strength.

Supersets

Performing two exercises back-to-back with no rest in between, usually targeting different muscle groups.
