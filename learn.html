Strength Training Basics

Strength training is a type of physical exercise specializing in the use of resistance to induce muscular contraction, which builds strength, anaerobic endurance, and size of skeletal muscles.

Key Principles:

  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increasing weight, reps, or sets to challenge your muscles
  • Proper Form and Technique: Ensuring correct movement patterns to prevent injury
  • Rest and Recovery: Allowing muscles time to repair and grow stronger
  • Consistency: Maintaining a regular training schedule

Basic Exercises:

Upper Body

  • • Bench Press
  • • Overhead Press
  • • Pull-ups
  • • Rows

Lower Body

  • • Squats
  • • Deadlifts
  • • Lunges
  • • Calf Raises

Training Frequency:

  • • Beginners: 2-3 times per week
  • • Intermediate: 3-4 times per week
  • • Advanced: 4-6 times per week
  • • Allow 48-72 hours between training the same muscle group

Nutrition Fundamentals

Proper nutrition is essential for muscle growth, recovery, and overall performance. Understanding macronutrients and their roles is crucial for achieving your fitness goals.

Macronutrients:

Proteins

  • • Building blocks for muscle
  • • 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight
  • • Sources: Chicken, fish, eggs, dairy

Carbohydrates

  • • Primary energy source
  • • 4-7g per kg bodyweight
  • • Sources: Rice, oats, potatoes, fruits

Fats

  • • Essential for hormone production
  • • 0.5-1.5g per kg bodyweight
  • • Sources: Avocados, nuts, olive oil

Meal Timing:

  • • Pre-workout: Carbs + protein 1-2 hours before
  • • Post-workout: Protein + carbs within 30 minutes
  • • Regular meals: Every 3-4 hours
  • • Stay hydrated: 3-4 liters of water daily

Recovery and Rest

Recovery is just as important as training itself. Proper rest and recovery techniques help prevent injury and optimize performance.

Recovery Methods:

Physical Recovery

  • • Sleep: 7-9 hours per night
  • • Stretching: 10-15 minutes daily
  • • Foam rolling: Target tight muscles
  • • Active recovery: Light cardio or mobility work

Recovery Techniques

  • • Ice baths: Reduce inflammation
  • • Massage: Improve blood flow
  • • Compression: Reduce swelling
  • • Proper nutrition: Support recovery

Injury Prevention:

  • • Warm up properly before training
  • • Use correct form and technique
  • • Don't increase weight too quickly
  • • Listen to your body's signals
  • • Take rest days when needed

Mental Strength

Success in fitness requires more than just physical strength. Developing the right mindset is crucial for long-term progress and consistency.

Key Mental Aspects:

Goal Setting

  • • Set SMART goals
  • • Break down long-term goals
  • • Track progress regularly
  • • Celebrate small wins

Motivation

  • • Find your "why"
  • • Create a routine
  • • Stay accountable
  • • Visualize success

Overcoming Challenges:

  • • Plateaus: Change your approach
  • • Lack of motivation: Remember your goals
  • • Time management: Prioritize and plan
  • • Setbacks: Learn and adapt
  • • Consistency: Build habits
